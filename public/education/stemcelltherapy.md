# Stem Cell Therapy

Stem cell therapy is a form of regenerative medicine that uses stem cells to repair or replace damaged tissues and cells in the body. Sources include **Mesenchymal Stem Cells (MSCs)**, **Hematopoietic Stem Cells**, and **Embryonic Stem Cells**.  

Stem cell therapy for knees was one of the first applications in regenerative medicine. Many athletes—including Ryan Tannehill, Kobe Bryant, Jamaal Charles, LaRon Landry, David Njoku, Chris Johnson, and Peyton Manning—chose MSCs for recovery. Peyton Manning had an NFL MVP season after using stem cells for his neck injury recovery, while Ryan Tannehill extended his career after ACL tear treatment.  

MSCs are multi-potent and can be derived from adipose tissue, umbilical cords, bone marrow, and even the placenta.  

An exciting development in the stem cell community is the use of stem cells for neurodegenerative diseases such as Autism. Although stem cell therapy can be costly, the potential benefits for Autism and other conditions are revolutionary. Our platform shares case studies to address long-term data concerns and offers apothecary reimbursements to reduce costs.
