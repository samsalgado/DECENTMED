# How to Accept Bitcoin Payments as a Business

Discover how to accept **Bitcoin payments** as a business and learn the best practices for secure transactions.

![Bitcoin](bitcoin)

Bitcoin is speculative to some and hope for others. Its price has skyrocketed in recent years, and many businesses want to accept it but aren’t sure how. Unlike stocks, Bitcoin isn’t traded via candlesticks, and transaction confirmation times can complicate merchant use.  

---

## Understanding Bitcoin & Inflation

- Bitcoin is **internationally recognized**, but regulations vary by country.  
- **Layer 1 Bitcoin transactions** take about 10 minutes to confirm—too slow for many merchants.  
- **Lightning Network** enables faster, cheaper Bitcoin transactions suitable for businesses.  

---

## Step 1: Choose a Wallet or Payment Processor

You have options depending on your needs:

- **Swiss Bitcoin Pay** – available in the US. Download via Google Play or Apple Store, set up your business account, and link your credentials.  
- **Crypto.com** – allows conversion to local currency, works like online banking, and pairs with US bank accounts.  
- **Muun Wallet** – self-custody wallet; gives full control over your Bitcoin keys.  
- **Other international wallets** – Blink and Wallet of Satoshi are user-friendly outside the US.  

---

## Step 2: Set Up Bitcoin & Lightning Addresses

- Create both a **Bitcoin address** and a **Lightning Network address**.  
- Consider a **cold wallet** for savings (offline device for maximum security).  
- Popular cold wallets: **Coldcard**, **Trezor**, **Ledger**.

## Step 3: Accept Payments & Protect Funds

1. Accept Bitcoin via **Lightning Network** using wallets like Muun, Swiss Bitcoin Pay, or Blink.  
2. Use a **cold wallet** for long-term savings and protection from online hacks.  

---

Bitcoin allows businesses to gain **control over their funds**, accept international payments, and prepare for a future where cryptocurrency may play a major role in commerce.
